Dome of the Rock

Masjid Al-Aqsa

  • Basic Structure: The structure is basically octagonal. It is capped at its centre by a dome.The octagonal arcade and the inner circular drum create an inner ambulatorium that encircles the holy rock.Both the circular drum and the exterior walls contain many windows.
  • Interior Design: The interior of the dome is lavishly decorated with mosaic, faience and marble, much of which was added several centuries after its completion.
  • Exterior decoration: The decoration of the outer walls went through two major phases: the initial Umayyad scheme comprised marble and mosaics, much like the interior walls. 16th-century Ottoman sultan Suleyman the Magnificent replaced it with Turkish faience tiles. The Ottoman tile decoration was replaced in the 1960s with faithful copies produced in Italy.
